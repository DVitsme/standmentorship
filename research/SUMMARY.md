## 1. Snapshot

**S.T.A.N.D Mentorship** is a small, founder-led **501(c)(3) youth-mentorship nonprofit** in **Maryland**, serving **young men of color** across **Baltimore City, Baltimore County, and Howard County**. It runs **free** "Leadership Academy" programs built on four pillars — **entrepreneurship/financial literacy, overall health, life skills, and skilled trades** — with an explicit mission of breaking cycles of violence and building leaders. Today it operates largely **in partnership with / under the umbrella of Columbia Community Care**, funded mainly by **Howard County public grants**.

It is **early-stage and very small** (files an IRS **990-N e-postcard**, i.e. **<$50K** gross receipts), but **punches above its weight** in local civic standing: the founder sits on the Howard County Executive's youth council, and the org was the **first-ever grantee** of the Black Philanthropy Circle of Howard County.

> **Tagline / founder's signature line:** *"You have not lived your life until you have changed one."* — Tigana S. Duncan

## 3. History & evolution — the org has transformed dramatically

This is one of the most important findings for storytelling. (Detail: `website/08-site-history-and-evolution.md`.)

- **2014:** Earliest recorded activity — a S.T.A.N.D Orioles game night in Baltimore. Documented operating history begins here.
- **2017–2018 — "boys-only after-school program" era.** S.T.A.N.D was an after-school mentoring program for **young men** in **Baltimore City**: Mon–Fri **2:45–4:45pm**, tied to the Baltimore City Schools calendar, **partnered with the Eastside Colts youth football team**, with **7 focus areas** (Mentoring/Community Service, Motivational Seminars, Overall Health, Academics/Tutoring, Crisis/Peer Support, Talent Development, Sport Training). 501(c)(3) granted April 2017; first website live (custom HTML, a promo video, and Twitter/YouTube/Google+/FB/IG links).
- **~2015:** Expanded into **Howard County** (per founder research).
- **~2021–2022:** Migrated to **Wix**; rebuilt around the current **Leadership Academy / 4-pillar** model (co-ed framing, regional, trades-forward).
- **2023–2026 — growth era.** Deepened ties to **Columbia Community Care**; won **Howard County** grants; founder joined the **YES! Council**; secured the **Black Philanthropy Circle**'s first grant; launched **"STAND Gear"** merch (Oct 2025). Site refreshed again between Feb 2025 and Jan 2026.

**Redesign hook:** A genuine *"from one after-school room to a regional leadership pipeline"* arc — made tangible by program **alumnus Darryl Jeffries Jr.**, who came through the program as a boy and now leads it as Program Director.

## 4. Mission, vision & programs

**Vision (verbatim):** *"Our vision is to be committed to strengthening, training, advancing, nurturing, and developing leaders who can continue a rippling effect of change for generations to come."*

**Mission (verbatim):** *"To empower individuals through transformative learning experiences and exceptional opportunities for educational and professional growth, ultimately enriching their lives."*

**The 4 Pillars (current model) — all offered at NO COST, staggered classes up to 6 days/week:**
1. **Entrepreneurship / Business Management** — financial literacy: passive income, budgeting, investments, credit 101.
2. **Overall Health** — physical, mental, spiritual wellness; healthy decision-making in the transition to adulthood. *(The one pillar carried over from the original 2017 model.)*
3. **Life Skills** — communication, problem-solving, emotional intelligence, time management, resilience, etc. — to be "marketable in the job market" or start a business.
4. **Trades & Innovation** — **plumbing, electrical, carpentry, coppersmith, barbering, CDL, HVAC**, plus interview/resume/job-readiness coaching.

**Delivery:** Two **Leadership Academy** cohorts — **Baltimore** and **Howard County** — with enrollment via Google Forms. The trades emphasis is **philosophy-driven** (exposing middle-schoolers to trades + college early), **not** a reflection of the founder's own career.

## 6. Partnerships, funding & impact

### Partnerships (HIGH unless noted) — detail in `external/03-partnerships-funding-events.md`
- **Columbia Community Care (CCC)** — **primary umbrella/host**; STAND won CCC's **"Outstanding Partnership Award 2024."** Much of STAND's Howard County operation runs with/through CCC (possible fiscal-sponsorship dynamic).
- **PUSH (Saquan Maxwell)** — sibling youth program; joint **"United We STAND"** summer program (2025).
- **Howard County Government** — founder on the **YES! Council**; a permanent space at **"The Source"**; works with the **courts, State's Attorney, and Sheriff** on recidivism reduction.
- **Wilde Lake Middle School (HCPSS)** — program site (not a district-wide MOU).
- **We Our Us / Bmore Men United (Dr. Andrey Bundley)** — co-host of the Baltimore father-son/Unity basketball game *(Medium-High)*.

### Funding (the org's growth engine — but immature)
- **Howard County government = dominant funder:** YEP! grant recipient; **$100,000** current-year + **~$250,000** prior two years for **"STAND & PUSH" jointly, via CCC**.
- **Black Philanthropy Circle of Howard County** (a fund of the Community Foundation of Howard County): **first-ever grant**, Dec 2025/Jan 2026 (amount undisclosed) — a notable honor.
- **Howard County Citizens Association**: a $500 community gift.
- **Confirmed absent** (searched, none found): major foundations (Horizon, Weinberg, Abell, France-Merrick, T. Rowe Price, CareFirst, BCF, United Way); **no corporate sponsors**; **no** GoFundMe/Givebutter/Eventbrite/galas; **no formal Ravens/Orioles partnership** (those were youth *outings*). Fundraising = a bare Wix donation form only.

### Impact / outcomes (cited by county & partner reports — HIGH)
- Summer program: **96% attendance**, **92% reported feeling "more confident."**
- Anti-violence framing with reported **zero re-arrest** outcomes among participants.
- Programs **free**, up to **6 days/week**.

## 7. Reputation, media & a sensitive context point

**Independent press coverage is thin** (detail: `external/02-news-media-awards.md`). The verifiable footprint is mostly **Howard County government** press releases, **Columbia Community Care**'s blog (a partner, not independent journalism), and **WMAR-2** father-son-basketball segments (~2021, 2022). **Nothing** found in Baltimore Sun/Banner/AFRO/WBAL/WJZ/FOX45; no podcasts, "40 Under 40," or proclamations.

**Handle with care:** In **July 2024, a 17-year-old S.T.A.N.D mentee, Angelo Little, was murdered at the Mall in Columbia** (covered by CBS Baltimore; Duncan was his mentor and spoke publicly). This tragedy is real, sensitive, and clearly central to the org's anti-violence mission and its deepened county support. **In any redesign, reference it only with dignity and restraint, ideally with the family's/org's blessing — never as a marketing device.**

## 10. Redesign-relevant takeaways

1. **Lead with the transformation story** (after-school room → regional leadership pipeline) and the **alumnus-now-leader** proof point.
2. **Surface impact & credibility** the current site hides: 96%/92% outcomes, zero re-arrest, county funding, BPC first-grant, CCC "Outstanding Partnership" award, delegate endorsement → an **Impact & Recognition** section.
3. **Make the trades/financial-literacy angle concrete and visual** — it's their genuine differentiator vs. generic "mentorship."
4. **Rebuild the donation experience** (tiers, recurring, EIN/501(c)(3) trust, "your gift funds X") to mature beyond county money toward foundations/corporates/individuals.
5. **Embed the Instagram feed; consolidate handles; add an org LinkedIn.**
6. **Turn the photo galleries into stories** (date, location, partners, a youth quote, an outcome).
7. **Clarify the CCC relationship** appropriately, and **add eligibility/"what to expect"** to enrollment.
8. **Audience clarity:** historically/explicitly **young men of color** — make the "who is this for, where, what age" unmistakable.
