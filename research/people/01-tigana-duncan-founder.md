## 1. Overview

Tigana S. Duncan is the founder and executive director of **S.T.A.N.D Mentorship**, legally **Stepping Towards A New Destiny Corporation**, a youth-mentorship nonprofit serving young men in **Baltimore City and Howard County, Maryland**. He is widely referred to as **"Coach Duncan."** He founded the program roughly a decade ago (earliest documented STAND event: 2014) and built it into a structured, no-cost workforce-development and mentoring program with a strong skilled-trades component.

- **CONFIRMED** — Founder/ED/CEO of STAND. (Org site; LinkedIn headline "Chief Executive Officer at STEPPING TOWARDS A NEW DESTINY CORPORATION"; Columbia Community Care; CBS Baltimore.)
- **CONFIRMED** — Born in **Trinidad & Tobago**. (Org "About" page; Columbia Community Care: "Born in Trinidad & Tobago, he learned firsthand the importance of family and community.")
- **CONFIRMED** — Known as **"Coach Duncan"**; a former **middle-school basketball coach** before/while leading STAND. (Columbia Community Care; WMAR-2; YouTube "Coach Tigana Duncan.")
- **UNVERIFIED** — Approximate age **~42** (born ~1983–1984). Sourced only to people-search/data-broker aggregators (Radaris/YellowBook-style summaries), which also list past Maryland locations of Baltimore, Westminster, and Catonsville. Treat as low-confidence and not independently confirmed.

## 5. Mentorship Philosophy (direct quotes)

All quotes below are tied to Duncan specifically.

- **Signature quote / site tagline:** *"You have not lived your life until you have changed one."* (Org homepage, attributed to Tigana S. Duncan.)
- *"It was life challenges and circumstances that created my desire to serve others."* (Org "About.")
- **On the trades focus (key quote):** *"By the time kids are in middle school, if you expose them to trades and innovation, and you expose them to college tours, now you are setting before them a path."* (CBS Baltimore, July 2024.) — This is the clearest articulation of why the program emphasizes trades.
- *"People need hope. They need a lifeline. Every kid is different."* (Columbia Community Care.)
- **On the United We STAND summer curriculum:** *"We wanted to know all their excuses for getting into trouble... Then we replaced all those reasons with opportunities."* (Columbia Community Care.)
- **On equity (YES! Council):** *"One of the things I find very important is equity for all kids... we can't forget that one percent of our kids that may not even have hope for tomorrow."* (Howard County.)
- **On male role models (father-son basketball):** *"They see men that come out here and support them. Not just their fathers, uncles, and grandfathers, but they got men they don't even know..."* (WMAR-2.)

> **Attribution caution:** A search-engine summary during research incorrectly attributed the quote *"In my lifetime, I've experienced some tough hardships and some beautiful blessings..."* to Duncan. Per the org's own About page, that quote belongs to **Jahlil Jarrett** (Director of Engagement), **not** Duncan. Excluded from Duncan's quotes above.

**Program model he built (CONFIRMED, org site):** no-cost, run as "Leadership Academy" cohorts in Baltimore and Howard County, organized around 4 pillars — (1) Entrepreneurship/Financial Literacy, (2) Overall Health, (3) Life Skills (classes six days/week), (4) **Trades & Innovation** (plumbing, electrical, carpentry, coppersmith, barber services, CDL, HVAC). Signature touchpoints include father-son basketball games, pro-sports experiences (Orioles, Ravens training camp), and enrichment trips (NY/Philadelphia, Ocean City, camping).
