# Programs — The 4 Pillars of STAND Mentoring

**URL:** https://www.standmentorship.org/programs
**Scraped:** 2026-06-17

Programs are offered **at no cost**. Operated via two "Leadership Academy" cohorts (Baltimore and Howard County).

## Pillar 1 — Entrepreneurship / Business Management
- Training on "Financial Literacy and Business Management": passive income, budgeting, investments, credit basics.
- Belief: financial literacy enables individuals to *"avoid financial loss and attain financial goals."*

## Pillar 2 — Overall Health
- Helps adolescents establish healthy behavior patterns during the transition to adulthood.
- Wellness framed as *"a holistic integration of physical, mental, and spiritual well-being."*

## Pillar 3 — Life Skills
- Soft-skills development to make participants *"marketable in the job market"* or able to start their own business.
- Operates **staggered class schedules, six days a week**, at **no cost**.
- Skills covered: effective communication, interpersonal skills, decision-making, problem-solving, creative thinking, critical thinking, self-awareness, empathy, flexibility, emotional intelligence, time management, assertiveness, resilience, handling criticism, self-management, cooperation.

## Pillar 4 — Trades & Innovation
- Vocational training in: **plumbing, electrical services, carpentry, coppersmith work, barber services, commercial driving (CDL), and HVAC technician work.**
- Additional coaching: job interviewing, application completion, resume writing, discovering talents, stress management.

## Observations for redesign
- This is a comprehensive, workforce-development-oriented youth program — not just "mentorship" in the abstract. The trades focus (HVAC, CDL, plumbing, electrical, barbering) is concrete and differentiating.
- "No cost" and "six days a week" are strong proof points that should be surfaced prominently.
