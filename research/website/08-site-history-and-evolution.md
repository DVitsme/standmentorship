## ORIGINAL program model (2017–2018) — a very different organization

In 2017–2018, **S.T.A.N.D was an after-school mentoring program specifically for YOUNG MEN (boys)** in **Baltimore City**. This is materially different from today's co-ed "Leadership Academy / 4 Pillars" model and explains Darryl Jeffries Jr.'s bio ("one of the first group of **boys** to come through the S.T.A.N.D program").

### Original "Major Basis" — 7 focus areas (verbatim, from `mentorship.html`, 2018)
1. **Mentoring (Community Service)** — "Provide positive role models for the young men to model themselves after... We also require that these young men participate in community service."
2. **Motivational Seminars/Engagements** — guest speakers "that have advanced in spite of their circumstances."
3. **Overall Health** — smart decisions during the transition from childhood to adulthood. *(This is the ONE pillar that survived into the current 4-pillar model.)*
4. **Academics/Tutoring** — "Education is number one."
5. **Crisis/Peer Support** — "short-term intervention to help each young man receive the support and the necessary resources needed."
6. **Talent Development** — "Each child has untapped potential."
7. **Sport Training** — **"We have partnered with the Eastside Colts Football team"** providing physical-activity opportunities. *(Concrete early partnership.)*

### Original program operations (verbatim highlights, from `programs.html`, 2018)
- **After-school, Monday–Friday, 2:45pm–4:45pm.**
- Tied to **Baltimore City Schools** calendar (closed when BCPS closed / inclement weather).
- Daily schedule: 2:45–3:30 Homework/Tutoring → 3:30–4:15 Mentorship → 4:15–4:45 Physical Activity/Talent Development → 4:45 Dismissal.
- Cognitive-based curriculum "free of stereotypes"; focus on physical, emotional, intellectual and social skills.
- Staff: school teachers, trained mentors, volunteers, coaches; **all background-checked**; some CPR/First Aid trained.
- Strict checkout/pickup policy; **late pick-up fee of $1/minute** after 4:50pm.
- Open-door communication policy with Program Directors.

### Original digital presence (2017)
- Promo video on homepage; aggressive social CTAs: *"As we are constantly... engaged with our Media, TV, and Digital family we decided to Utilize our FACEBOOK for almost everything."*
- Linked **Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, YouTube, and Google+**. (Today only Facebook + Instagram are linked — Twitter/YouTube/Google+ presence appears abandoned; verify with social-media research.)

## Evolution summary (for the redesign narrative)
- **Then (≈2014–2018):** A grassroots, Baltimore City, boys-only, after-school mentoring program tied to schools and a youth football team.
- **Now (2022–2026):** A broader, co-ed, **free Leadership Academy** across Baltimore + Howard County with a workforce/**trades** emphasis (HVAC, CDL, plumbing, electrical, carpentry, barbering), financial literacy, life skills, and health — governed by a formal **board of directors**.
- The redesign can powerfully tell this **"full-circle growth" story**: from one after-school room to a regional leadership pipeline, with alumni (e.g., Darryl Jeffries Jr.) now leading.
